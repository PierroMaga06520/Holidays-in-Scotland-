Day by Day
1
Day

Arrival — Loch Lomond

Monday, 27 July
Arrival Loch Lomond 1 Night · ✅ Confirmed

Land at Edinburgh Airport and head straight west — skip the city for now and drive the A82 along the eastern shore of Loch Lomond to Tarbet. A beautiful 1.5h drive that eases you straight into the Highland landscape. Glencoe is already calling from the north.

🏨 The Tarbet Hotel ✅ Booked · £128.38

A traditional Scottish lochside hotel right on the banks of Loch Lomond in the village of Tarbet. Classic Highland hospitality with stunning loch views, a welcoming bar and restaurant, and the perfect first taste of the Highlands after landing.

Tarbet, Loch Lomond · 27–28 July · £128.38 confirmed

🌊 Evening Tip

Stroll down to the Loch Lomond shore at sunset — the view across the water to the hills is spectacular. The hotel bar is a great spot to unwind with your first dram of the trip.

2
Day

Drive to Glencoe — Ballachulish

Tuesday, 28 July
~1h Drive Ballachulish · 2 Nights · ✅ Confirmed

Continue north on the A82 through Rannoch Moor — one of the wildest and most desolate landscapes in Britain. The first sight of Glencoe emerging from the moor is breathtaking. Ballachulish sits right at the gateway to Glencoe, at the foot of Loch Linnhe.

🏨 The Ballachulish Hotel ✅ Booked · £559.44

A grand Victorian hotel sitting directly on the shores of Loch Linnhe, with Glencoe's dramatic mountains as a backdrop. Spacious rooms with loch views, a whisky bar, and a restaurant serving Scottish produce. The perfect base for two days of Glencoe exploration.

Ballachulish, Glencoe · 28–30 July · £559.44 confirmed (2 nights)

💡 Stop en Route

Pause at the Glencoe Visitor Centre (National Trust) to learn about the 1692 Massacre of Glencoe — the valley's dark history makes every view more haunting and beautiful.

3
Day

Hike #1 — Glencoe Lochan & Steall Falls

Wednesday, 29 July
🥾 Day Hike Glencoe
🗻 Morning: Glencoe Lochan Loop

An easy 1.5 km circular trail starting from the village. The lochan perfectly mirrors every mountain and pine tree around it — absolutely stunning, especially in morning light. Allow 45 minutes at a leisurely pace.

Easy · 1.5 km · 45 min · Free parking in Glencoe village

💧 Afternoon: Steall Falls & Nevis Gorge

Drive 20 minutes to Glen Nevis and follow this dramatic 4 km out-and-back trail through a narrow, rocky gorge to reach Scotland's second-highest waterfall — a 120m cascade that tumbles into a meadow below Ben Nevis. Optional wire-rope bridge crossing for the brave.

Moderate · 4 km return · ~2h · Glen Nevis car park (paid)

🍺 Evening

Back to The Ballachulish Hotel for dinner — their restaurant serves excellent Scottish produce with those unforgettable loch views. Order the local venison or fresh Loch Linnhe seafood with a dram by the fire.

4
Day

Drive to Isle of Skye

Thursday, 30 July
~2h Drive Skye · 1 Night · ✅ Confirmed

Head north to Fort William then west along the legendary Road to the Isles (A830) — one of the most beautiful drives in Britain. Cross onto Skye via the Skye Bridge. Stop at Eilean Donan Castle on the way for a classic photograph.

🏨 Greshornish House Hotel ✅ Booked · £378

A stunning early 18th-century country house tucked away on the remote Greshornish Peninsula, overlooking its own private loch. One of Skye's most secluded and romantic hideaways — candlelit dining, log fires, and total peace. Only 8 rooms, deeply atmospheric.

Greshornish, Isle of Skye · 30–31 July · £378 confirmed (1 night)

🦅 Wildlife Watch

Keep eyes peeled on the drive — golden eagles and red deer are often spotted near Glen Shiel. The Greshornish Peninsula itself is excellent for otters and sea eagles from the shoreline.

5
Day

Hike #2 — Quiraing, Isle of Skye → Inverness

Friday, 31 July
🥾 Day Hike ~2.5h Drive Inverness · 2 Nights · ✅ Confirmed

Check out of Greshornish and head up to the Trotternish Peninsula before driving to Inverness. An action-packed day — Skye's most surreal terrain in the morning, then the Highland capital by evening.

🗻 Morning: The Quiraing Circuit

A 7 km circular route through an otherworldly landscape of soaring cliffs, undulating green slopes, hidden plateaux, and needle-like pinnacles. The trail offers sweeping views across the Sound of Raasay. Arrive early to beat the crowds and catch the morning mist lifting.

Moderate · 7 km · ~3–4h · Parking at Quiraing car park (small fee)

🪨 Option: Old Man of Storr

Only 20 minutes south — a shorter but iconic hike (3.8 km, 1.5h) to the dramatic rock pinnacles of the Storr. Do this if time allows before the drive to Inverness.

Easy–Moderate · 3.8 km · ~1.5h · Storr car park (paid)

🏨 Hootananny ✅ Booked · £752.64

Yes — you've booked rooms at the legendary Hootananny, Inverness's most celebrated live music pub and hotel! Traditional stone building in the heart of the city, pulsing with folk sessions every evening. You're staying above the party — earplugs optional, atmosphere guaranteed.

67 Church St, Inverness · 31 July–2 Aug · £752.64 confirmed (2 nights)

🎶 Evening

Walk downstairs to the bar — live Scottish folk sessions start around 9pm. You literally don't have to go anywhere. This is one of the great lucky coincidences of the trip!

Inverness — Loch Ness & Rest

Saturday, 1 August
Inverness Night 2 at Hootananny · ✅ Confirmed

A full day in and around Inverness — drive the shores of Loch Ness, explore the city, and enjoy another evening of live folk music downstairs at Hootananny.

🏨 Hootananny — Night 2 ✅ Confirmed

Your second night at Hootananny. Saturday sessions are usually the liveliest of the week — expect a packed room, folk music and dancing until late. A perfect Highland finale before heading south tomorrow.

67 Church St, Inverness · Included in 31 July–2 Aug booking

🏰 Day Trip: Urquhart Castle & Loch Ness

The lakeside castle ruins on Loch Ness are hauntingly beautiful — walk the grounds, take in the view, and keep an eye on the water. Then drive to the Dores Inn on the south shore of Loch Ness for a pint with a view. Admission ~£15pp.

🥃 Whisky Option

Dalwhinnie Distillery is only 1 hour south on the A9 — a beautiful drive that previews tomorrow's route to Edinburgh. Perfect for an afternoon tasting before returning to Inverness for the evening.

Day

Hike #3 — Cairngorms en route South

Sunday, 2 August
🥾 Day Hike ~3h Drive total Edinburgh · ✅ Confirmed

Check out of Hootananny and drive south through the Cairngorms — stop for a morning hike, then continue to Edinburgh to check in by afternoon. Frederick House Hotel is waiting.

🌲 Morning: Loch an Eilein Circuit

A perfect 7 km circular walk around a tranquil loch with a fairytale ruined island castle, framed by ancient Scots pines. Mostly level, great for all abilities. One of Scotland's most beautiful and atmospheric walks. The two-hour loop fits perfectly before continuing south.

Easy · 7 km loop · ~2h · Rothiemurchus estate near Aviemore (paid parking)

💧 Bonus: Loch Morlich Wild Swim

Only 15 minutes from Loch an Eilein — sandy beaches, shallow entry, stunning Cairngorm mountain backdrop. A perfect refreshing dip before the final drive to Edinburgh. Café next door for a warming drink after.

Easy · Right off the road · 5 min from car park · Aviemore area

🏨 Frederick House Hotel, Edinburgh ✅ Booked · £328.95

A stylish Georgian townhouse hotel right in the heart of Edinburgh's New Town, steps from Princes Street and the castle. A wonderful base for your final evening in the city — elegant rooms, great location, and Edinburgh on your doorstep.

Frederick Street, Edinburgh · Check-in 2 Aug evening · £328.95 confirmed

Edinburgh Frederick House · ✅ Confirmed

Harry Potter Magic

🚂 Glenfinnan Viaduct — The Hogwarts Bridge

One of Scotland's most iconic sights — the 21-arch curved viaduct soaring 30m over the Glencoe valley, featured in Chamber of Secrets and Prisoner of Azkaban. The Jacobite Steam Train (the real-life Hogwarts Express) crosses it at ~10:45am and ~1:15pm daily from April to October. Arrive 45 min early and hike 15 min up to the hilltop viewpoint for the shot. Park at the NTS car park (£3.50). Just 25 min from Fort William — perfect en route from Ballachulish to Skye on Day 4.

📍 Glenfinnan, A830 · On your Day 4 route to Skye · Book Jacobite train tickets months ahead if you want to ride

🪄 Loch Eilt — Dumbledore's Grave

Just a 5-minute stop off the A830, barely 10 minutes past Glenfinnan. A small island in Loch Eilt was used as Dumbledore's burial place. You can see it clearly from the roadside. Combine with Glenfinnan for a full Potter pilgrimage in one morning.

📍 A830 roadside, between Glenfinnan and Lochailort · Free · 5 min walk from lay-by

💧 Steall Falls, Glen Nevis — Triwizard Tournament

The meadow at the foot of Steall Falls was the filming location for the Triwizard Tournament in Goblet of Fire. You're already hiking here on Day 3 — keep your eyes open for that magical grassy amphitheatre beneath the falls.

📍 Glen Nevis, near Fort William · Already in your Day 3 hike itinerary

🥃

Whisky Distillery Visits

🏭 Talisker Distillery — Isle of Skye

Scotland's most famous island distillery, perched on the wild shores of Loch Harport with the Cuillin Hills as a backdrop. Talisker produces a bold, briny, peppery single malt shaped by the sea air — their "Made by the Sea" motto is no marketing trick. The tour walks you through copper pot stills and traditional worm tubs, finishing with a proper tasting. Book in advance — it sells out in summer.

📍 Carbost, Isle of Skye · ~£20pp · taliskerwhisky.com · 20 min from Portree · Best on Day 4 or 5

🏭 Dalwhinnie Distillery — Cairngorms

Scotland's highest distillery at 326m, sitting in the hills just off the A9 near Aviemore. Dalwhinnie produces a gentle, honeyed Highland malt — very approachable for non-whisky-lovers. The guide-led tours are well-reviewed for being fun and informative, and they offer mini bottles to take home if you're driving. Ideal on Day 7, perfectly on route between Inverness and the Cairngorms.

📍 Dalwhinnie, near Aviemore · ~£15pp · malts.com/dalwhinnie · Book ahead · Perfect on Day 7 route

🍺

Outstanding Pubs

🏴󠁧󠁢󠁳󠁣󠁴󠁿 The Drovers Inn — Inverarnan, Loch Lomond

Founded in 1705, this is one of the most extraordinary pubs in Scotland. Stepping inside feels like time-travel — the decor is virtually unchanged for 300 years, complete with taxidermied animals including a full stuffed grizzly bear, dark wooden beams, and an open fire. Frequented by Highland cattle drovers and now by walkers on the West Highland Way. Rumoured to be haunted. Stop here for lunch on Day 2 as you drive north from Loch Lomond toward Glencoe — it's right on the A82.

📍 Inverarnan, Loch Lomond · On the A82 · Perfect Day 2 lunch stop · droversinn.co.uk

🏴󠁧󠁢󠁳󠁣󠁴󠁿 The Stein Inn — Waternish, Isle of Skye

The oldest pub on Skye, tucked at the end of a winding coastal road on the remote Waternish Peninsula. A shining beacon of hospitality: real ales on tap, over 90 single malts behind the bar, a roaring fire, and views across the sea loch. Locals and hikers mix easily. The detour is worth every mile. Pair it with sunset — the sky over the water from here is breathtaking.

📍 Stein, Waternish Peninsula, Skye · 30 min from Dunvegan · Best visited Day 4 evening or Day 5

🏴󠁧󠁢󠁳󠁣󠁴󠁿 Hootananny — Inverness

A legendary live music pub in the heart of Inverness, pulsing with energy every evening. Traditional Scottish folk sessions downstairs, a rooftop bar above, and a warm, unpretentious atmosphere that mixes locals and travellers perfectly. Don't plan an early night — things don't get going until 9pm.

📍 67 Church St, Inverness · Free entry most nights · hootanannyinverness.co.uk · Perfect for Night 5

🏊

Wild Swimming

Scottish water is cold even in July — 10–14°C in most lochs. Take it slow getting in, never swim alone, and bring a dry robe or towel. The cold is absolutely worth it.

💧 The Fairy Pools — Isle of Skye

Crystal-clear turquoise pools fed by waterfalls at the foot of the Black Cuillin mountains near Glenbrittle — one of the most magical wild swimming spots in the UK. You can swim through a natural arch underwater (bring goggles). Go very early morning to beat the crowds and have the pools to yourself in the mist. A wetsuit is advisable but not essential.

📍 Glenbrittle, Isle of Skye · 25 min walk from car park · Best on Day 4 or 5 · Free · Go before 8am

💧 River Etive — Glen Etive, Glencoe

A single-track road off the A82 follows the River Etive through one of Scotland's wildest and most dramatic glens. There are hundreds of natural pools, cascades and gentle slides along the river — just stop anywhere and jump in. Wild, remote, and utterly magical. This road was a filming location for Skyfall (James Bond). Allow a full afternoon.

📍 Glen Etive road off the A82, near Glencoe · Best on Day 3 afternoon · Free · No facilities

💧 Loch Morlich — Cairngorms

The most accessible and beginner-friendly wild swimming loch in the Highlands, with sandy beaches, shallow entry points, and a stunning backdrop of Cairngorm Mountain. Crystal-clear water, a watersports centre café nearby, and enough room to swim without feeling crowded. Perfect after the Loch an Eilein hike on Day 7 — the two are only 15 minutes apart.

📍 Glenmore, near Aviemore · Car park at loch · Café next door · Perfect on Day 7 afternoon
